What is DbSee ?

DbSee is an yet another Database Browser, but with some nice features. I originally developed it for my personal use in Software Design, Development and Testing. Hoping that it may be useful to others, I have put together a nicer and freeware version of it as DbSee.


What it Can Do

You can use DbSee to connect to a variety of Databases (depending on the installed Drivers) and

  • Explore Tables and Views.
  • Write and Execute your own Queries  and save them conveniently into a single file for later use.
  • Open Table/View/Query, optionally with Filtering and display their Data in Grid.
    • Grid Data can be Grouped/Filtered/Sorted and Exported as Excel/XML/Html/Text file.
    • Memo and Blob/Picture Fields are displayed separately.
    • You can also Edit, Append, Delete the data displayed.
    • When a Table, View or Query is opened, it is displayed separately under a tab in the viewing area making it easy to switch between them.
  • For Interbase, MS SQL and Access databases (and possibly other Databases accessed through ADO Drivers) DbSee will extract and display Primary and Foreign Key information and show the Tables in a Tree-Grid, depicting Master-Detail (One to Many) Relationships. When a Detail Table (at a child node) are opened, it shows the data corresponding its Master Record. You can browse Master-Detail data with ease up to a depth of 2 ( Master ¢ Detail ¢ Sub-Detail ).
  • The current record of data, displayed in the grid can also be viewed in a Record View - a grid with two columns Column Name & Value. This Record View is also extended to viewing many to one relations - for e.g while viewing the data of a Products table, you can lookup the record from Supplier table, in Record View, that corresponds to the SupplierID of the current record of Products.


What it Cannot Do

  • DbSee is not an Administrative tool - You cannot use it to Create, Backup or Modify Structure of a Database.
  • DbSee has no Printing functionality. However you can export the displayed data to MS Excel and print it from there.
  • It does not have a Query Designer now (but has a syntax highlighted SQL Editor) - May be in the future ?
